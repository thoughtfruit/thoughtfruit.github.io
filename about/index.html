Creator of Open Opportunities

The Open Opportunities Platform is an open-source platform that was built to allow Government agencies to collaborate on projects.

The goal is to allow Government agencies to break out of the top-down silos where they only collaborate with other people in their agency, and allow for cross-agency productivity.

I architected and built the first version with a co-creator, and continue to be an active member in the open source development thereof (albeit not as much lately).

Since it's roll-out in 2014, it's been very successful. For example: we allowed the CIA to collaborate with Embassy staff in Iran & Africa, while they both work with the Red Cross and Department of State to get a project across the finish line. In the private sector, this seems completely normal, but this is a radical of a transformation for the US Government.

Hundreds of programmers have contributed to the ecosystem of development needs via Github, and served as the impetus for the founding of the 18F Government Agency and the United States Digital Service.
